About me

Hello! Nice to meet you.

◎ images/profile.jpg

My name is HyungCheol Kim, and I'm a Biomedical Engineer at Applied Medical, where I work on process engineering for medical device manufacturing. I graduated from UC Irvine in June 2025 with a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering, specializing in Biophotonics.

At UCI, I had the opportunity to work in the CERVOS lab, where I used CT scans and 3D Slicer to measure cervical anatomy and silicone molding to develop a novel patient-specific pessary device that addresses women's health. I also led the hardware design for REACH, my senior design project focused on building a rehabilitative device for stroke survivors, which earned the Dean's Choice Award. These experiences solidified my interest in medical imaging, device design, and bringing ideas from concept to physical prototype.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy hitting the gym, snowboarding, and woodworking.

I'm excited to keep growing and find new opportunities that combine hands-on engineering with medical imaging and device innovation!
