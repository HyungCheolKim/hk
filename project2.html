REACH FES Device

★ Dean's Choice Award
◧ images/project-reach-hero.jpg

A wearable Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) device for muscle rehabilitation, controlled by a custom-designed Raspberry Pi fixture and integrated with real-time EMG feedback. Awarded the Dean's Choice Award through the REACH program.

Problem

FES devices stimulate muscle contractions electrically to assist patients recovering from neurological conditions. The challenge was building a compact, controllable housing that could interface a Raspberry Pi with the FES electronics reliably.

Hardware Design

Designed and 3D printed a custom fixture housing the Raspberry Pi, providing a secure, organized enclosure for all electronics. Led multiple design iterations to optimize fit, cable routing, and heat dissipation.

◧ Housing / 3D print photo images/project-reach-housing.jpg

EMG Integration

Collaborated with a multidisciplinary team to integrate real-time EMG feedback, allowing the device to modulate stimulation intensity based on measured muscle activation improving both safety and rehabilitation outcomes.

◧ Full system photo images/project-reach-system.jpg

Results

The device successfully demonstrated closed-loop FES control during the REACH showcase, earning the Dean's Choice Award at the UCI Samueli School of Engineering. The project was also published through eScholarship you can read the full paper below.
